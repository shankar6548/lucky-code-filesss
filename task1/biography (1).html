Biography

Nandamuri Balakrishna, also known as NBK or Balayya, is a popular Indian film actor and politician primarily active in the Telugu film industry. He was born on June 10, 1960, in Chennai (then Madras), Tamil Nadu, as the sixth son of the legendary actor and former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, N. T. Rama Rao. He completed his Bachelor of Commerce from Nizam College in Hyderabad. Balakrishna started his acting career as a child artist in the film Tatamma Kala (1974) at the age of 14. He made his debut as a lead actor with Sahasame Jeevitham in 1984 and has since appeared in over 100 films, known for his powerful performances in action and historical roles. He rose to stardom with movies like Simha, Legend, Akhanda, Veera Simha Reddy, and Bhagavanth Kesari. His energetic dialogue delivery, intense screen presence, and strong fan base made him one of the most bankable stars in Tollywood. Over the years, he has received several awards, including three Nandi Awards, three SIIMA Awards, and an IIFA Award for his contribution to cinema. He also hosted the talk show Unstoppable with NBK on the Aha platform, which gained high popularity. Apart from his film career, NBK is a senior member of the Telugu Desam Party (TDP). He entered politics in 2014 and has been elected twice as the Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) from the Hindupur constituency. He is also involved in social service and is the Chairman of the Basavatarakam Indo-American Cancer Hospital and Research Institute in Hyderabad, named after his mother. In 2025, he was honored with the Padma Bhushan, India’s third-highest civilian award, for his contributions to Indian cinema and public service. On the personal front, Balakrishna is married to Vasundhara Devi, and they have three children: two daughters, Brahmani and Tejaswini, and a son, Mokshagna Teja, who is expected to enter the film industry. Balakrishna remains an influential figure in both the entertainment and political fields of Andhra Pradesh.

Carrer

Nandamuri Balakrishna began his career in the Telugu film industry as a child actor in the film Tatamma Kala (1974), directed by his father, the legendary N. T. Rama Rao. He transitioned into lead roles in the early 1980s, with his debut as a hero in Sahasame Jeevitham (1984). Over the decades, he established himself as one of Tollywood's top mass heroes, known for high-energy action roles, powerful dialogues, and mythological/historical characters. He has acted in over 100 films, with major box-office hits like Mangammagari Manavadu, Narasimha Naidu, Simha, Legend, Akhanda, Veera Simha Reddy, and Bhagavanth Kesari. His unique screen presence, especially in mass and patriotic roles, earned him a strong fan base. He won several awards, including Nandi Awards, SIIMA Awards, and IIFA, and was recognized as a pillar of commercial Telugu cinema. Apart from films, he also gained popularity as the host of the talk show Unstoppable with NBK on Aha. In addition to his film success, Balakrishna entered politics in 2014 as a member of the Telugu Desam Party (TDP). He was elected as the MLA from Hindupur, a constituency previously held by his family. He balanced his film career and political responsibilities successfully. He is also involved in philanthropy, particularly as chairman of the Basavatarakam Indo-American Cancer Hospital in Hyderabad. His career continues to thrive both on-screen and in public life, making him a respected figure in Andhra Pradesh’s cinema and political landscape.

Achivements

Nandamuri Balakrishna has achieved great success both in the film industry and in public service. As an actor, he has starred in over 100 Telugu films across four decades, earning a reputation for his powerful dialogue delivery and mass appeal. His major blockbuster movies include Simha, Legend, Akhanda, and Veera Simha Reddy. He has received several awards, including three prestigious Nandi Awards, three SIIMA Awards, and an IIFA Utsavam Award, recognizing his talent and contribution to Telugu cinema. In 2025, he was honored with the Padma Bhushan, India’s third-highest civilian award, for his service in the fields of cinema and public life. In politics, Balakrishna has served as the MLA for Hindupur since 2014, representing the Telugu Desam Party (TDP). He is also known for his social service, particularly as the Chairman of the Basavatarakam Indo-American Cancer Hospital, which provides cancer treatment to thousands of patients. Additionally, his talk show Unstoppable with NBK became one of the most popular Telugu web shows, showcasing his versatility as a host. His ability to balance acting, politics, and philanthropy makes him one of the most respected personalities in Andhra Pradesh.

Goals

Nandamuri Balakrishna's goals have always been focused on serving the people, preserving his father's legacy, and contributing to Telugu cinema and society. In films, his goal is to entertain audiences with powerful roles and meaningful stories, especially ones that reflect Telugu culture, heroism, and tradition. As a politician, his main aim is to serve the people of his constituency, Hindupur, and support the growth and development of Andhra Pradesh through the Telugu Desam Party (TDP). In healthcare, he works to expand cancer treatment access through the Basavatarakam Indo-American Cancer Hospital, which he leads as chairman. He also aims to promote new talent in the Telugu film industry and preserve the values and achievements of his father, N. T. Rama Rao. Overall, Balakrishna's goals combine cinema, public service, and social welfare to make a lasting impact on the state and its people.
